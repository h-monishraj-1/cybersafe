Cybersecurity Awareness

Essential knowledge to protect yourself from common cyber threats and stay safe online.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where cybercriminals impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

How to Identify Phishing:

  • Urgent or threatening language demanding immediate action
  • Generic greetings like "Dear Customer" instead of your name
  • Suspicious email addresses that don't match the claimed sender
  • Links that don't match the legitimate website URL
  • Requests for sensitive information via email
  • Poor grammar and spelling mistakes

Protection Tips:

  • Always verify sender identity through independent channels
  • Hover over links to see the actual destination before clicking
  • Type URLs directly into your browser instead of clicking links
  • Use email filters and anti-phishing tools
  • Report suspicious emails to your IT department or email provider

Ransomware

Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts your files and demands payment for decryption. It's one of the most damaging types of cyberattacks affecting individuals and organizations.

How Ransomware Works:

  • Gains access through phishing emails, malicious downloads, or software vulnerabilities
  • Encrypts files on infected systems making them inaccessible
  • Displays ransom demands, usually requesting cryptocurrency payment
  • May threaten to delete files or publish sensitive data

Prevention Strategies:

  • Maintain regular, offline backups of important data
  • Keep operating systems and software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software with real-time protection
  • Avoid downloading software from untrusted sources
  • Implement network segmentation to limit spread
  • Train employees on cybersecurity best practices

Password Safety

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts and personal information.

Password Best Practices:

  • Use at least 12 characters with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Create unique passwords for each account
  • Use passphrases - combinations of random words are easier to remember
  • Avoid personal information like birthdays, names, or addresses
  • Don't use common passwords like "password123" or "qwerty"
  • Change default passwords on all devices and accounts

Password Management:

  • Use a reputable password manager to generate and store unique passwords
  • Enable automatic password generation for new accounts
  • Regularly audit and update old or weak passwords
  • Never share passwords via email or text messages
  • Use secure password recovery options

Two-Factor Authentication

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security by requiring two forms of verification to access your accounts.

Types of 2FA:

  • SMS/Text Messages: Codes sent to your phone number
  • Authenticator Apps: Time-based codes from apps like Google Authenticator
  • Hardware Tokens: Physical devices that generate codes
  • Biometric: Fingerprints, face recognition, or voice recognition
  • Push Notifications: Approve login attempts through mobile apps

Why 2FA is Important:

  • Protects accounts even if passwords are compromised
  • Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access
  • Required by many organizations for sensitive data access
  • Easy to set up and use on most platforms
  • Provides alerts when someone attempts to access your accounts

Best Practices:

  • Enable 2FA on all accounts that support it, especially email and financial accounts
  • Use authenticator apps instead of SMS when possible
  • Keep backup codes in a secure location
  • Register multiple devices for 2FA when available
  • Regularly review and update 2FA settings

Major Cyberattack Timeline

Understanding the history of major cyberattacks helps us learn from past incidents and prepare for future threats.

Equifax Data Breach

2017

Personal information of 147 million people compromised, including Social Security numbers and credit card data.

WannaCry Ransomware

2017

Global ransomware attack affecting over 300,000 computers across 150+ countries, including critical infrastructure.

SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack

2020

Sophisticated attack compromising software used by government agencies and major corporations worldwide.

Colonial Pipeline Ransomware

2021

DarkSide ransomware shut down the largest fuel pipeline in the US, causing widespread gas shortages.

Log4j Vulnerability

2021

Critical vulnerability in widely-used Java logging library affected millions of applications worldwide.

LastPass Data Breaches

2022

Password manager company suffered multiple breaches exposing encrypted password vaults of millions of users.
